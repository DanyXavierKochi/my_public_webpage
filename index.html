St. Antony’s Church, Kannamaly is a faith-filled parish with a sacred heritage spanning over 267 years. Rooted in devotion and shaped by resilience through history, the church stands today as a living testimony of God’s grace and the loving intercession of St. Antony, serving as a spiritual home for generations of faithful.

The Kannamaly St. Joseph Nercha Sadya, observed since 1905, commemorates a miraculous healing from a cholera outbreak and continues to draw lakhs of devotees every year on 19 March.

The Miraculous Statue of St. Joseph, brought from Spain in 1905, remains a treasured symbol of divine grace for the faithful.

History of St. Antony’s Church, Kannamaly

The first Kannamaly church was established in 1745 under the patronage of Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception. It was destroyed in a sea attack in 1795, leading to the construction of a second church in 1796.

In 1873, Kannamaly became an independent parish. Severe sea attacks in 1905 and 1908 caused major destruction, after which the present church foundation was laid on 13 June 1909.

The church was solemnly blessed on 22 November 1942 and is now revered under the patronage of St. Antony.

History of St. Joseph Nercha Sadya

Originating in 1905 during a devastating cholera outbreak, the Nercha Sadya began after a divine vision received by the parish priest, Fr. Joseph Swares.

Through the powerful intercession of St. Joseph, the epidemic subsided, and Kannamaly has since remained protected. The feast is observed every year on 19 March.

Miraculous Statue of St. Joseph

Brought from Spain in 1905, the statue miraculously remained at Kannamaly after a ship carrying it became grounded.

The statue soon became known for miracles, including the healing of cattle, and was later installed reverently in the church.

The New Chapel of St. Joseph

The foundation stone was laid on 19 March 2012 by Shri Oommen Chandy and blessed by Bishop Dr. Joseph Kariyil.

Blessed in March 2015, the chapel uniquely houses relics of ten saints and stands as the only such chapel in Asia.
