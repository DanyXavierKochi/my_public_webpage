St. Antony’s Church, Kannamaly is a faith-filled parish with a sacred heritage spanning over 281 years. Rooted in devotion and shaped by resilience through history, the Church stands today as a living testimony of God’s grace and the loving intercession of St. Antony, serving as a spiritual home for generations of faithful.

The Kannamaly St. Joseph Nercha Sadya, observed since 1905, commemorates a miraculous healing from a cholera outbreak. Held annually on 19th March, the feast remains a powerful expression of faith and thanksgiving.

The Miraculous Statue of St. Joseph of Kannamaly, brought from Spain in 1905, is a treasured sign of God’s grace, drawing countless devotees who seek his powerful intercession.

History of St. Antony’s Church, Kannamaly

St. Antony’s Church, Kannamaly has a rich and enduring history spanning over 281 years. The first Church was established in 1745, about 3 km west of the present Church site, under the patronage of Our Lady of Immaculate Conception.

This Church was destroyed by sea erosion in 1795, following which a second Church was constructed in 1796. In 1873, Kannamaly Church was separated from the Mundamvely parish and declared an independent parish.

Severe sea erosions in 1905 and 1908 caused partial damage and complete ruin of the Church. On June 13, 1909, the foundation stone for the third Church was laid at the present site.

The present Church, modeled after St. Paul’s Basilica of Rome, was solemnly blessed on November 22, 1942 and is now revered under the patronage of St. Antony.

St. Joseph Feast & Nercha Sadya

The Nercha Sadya Feast of St. Joseph at Kannamaly dates back to 1905, during a period marked by severe sea erosion and a devastating cholera outbreak.

Rev. Fr. Joseph Suarez received a divine vision of St. Joseph with the Infant Jesus and Blessed Virgin Mary. Following this, food prepared in homes was blessed and shared.

Through the powerful intercession of St. Joseph, the cholera epidemic was brought under control, and Kannamaly has since been spared from the disease.

Every year on March 19, lakhs of devotees gather to participate in this sacred offering with faith and devotion.

Miraculous Statue of St. Joseph

The miraculous statue of St. Joseph was brought from Spain in 1905 by Carmelite priests. When the ship carrying the statue reached Kannamaly, it mysteriously grounded.

One statue was taken ashore, after which the ship floated free. Attempts to reload the statue failed, and it was recognized as divine will.

Miracles soon followed, including the healing of cattle using straw that had wrapped the statue. The statue was later repaired and installed in the Church.

Miraculous Shrine of St. Joseph

The foundation stone for the Miraculous Shrine of St. Joseph was laid on March 18, 2012. The shrine combines Gothic, Roman, and contemporary architectural styles.

Built over three floors, the shrine houses relics of ten saints and was solemnly blessed on March 8, 2015. It is the only shrine in Asia to enshrine ten saints under one roof.
