St. Antony’s Church, Kannamaly is a faith-filled parish with a sacred heritage spanning over 281 years. Rooted in devotion and shaped by resilience through history, the Church stands today as a living testimony of God’s grace and the loving intercession of St. Antony.

The Kannamaly St. Joseph Nercha Sadya, observed since 1905, commemorates a miraculous healing from a cholera outbreak. Held annually on 19th March, the feast remains a powerful expression of faith and thanksgiving.

The Miraculous Statue of St. Joseph of Kannamaly, brought from Spain in 1905, is a treasured sign of God’s grace, drawing countless devotees who seek his powerful intercession.

History of St. Antony’s Church

The history of St. Antony’s Church, Kannamaly dates back to 1745 when the first church was constructed under the patronage of Our Lady of Immaculate Conception.

Due to severe sea erosion, the church was destroyed in 1795, leading to the construction of a second church in 1796. In 1873, Kannamaly became an independent parish.

Further sea erosion in 1905 and 1908 caused complete destruction. The foundation stone for the present church was laid on June 13, 1909.

The present church, modeled after St. Paul’s Basilica of Rome, was solemnly blessed on November 22, 1942.

History of the Kannamaly St. Joseph Nercha Sadya

The Nercha Sadya Feast of St. Joseph originated in 1905 during a severe cholera epidemic.

Rev. Fr. Joseph Suarez received a divine vision of St. Joseph with Infant Jesus and the Blessed Virgin Mary.

After the blessed meal was shared, the epidemic subsided completely. Since then, the feast is observed annually on March 19.

Miraculous Statue of St. Joseph

The miraculous statue of St. Joseph was brought from Spain by Carmelite priests in 1905.

When the ship grounded near Kannamaly, one statue was taken ashore and could not be moved again.

Later, cattle afflicted by disease were healed using straw from the statue, revealing its miraculous power.

The Miraculous Shrine of St. Joseph

The original chapel built in 1946 bore the marks of time.

The foundation stone for the new shrine was laid on March 18, 2012.

The shrine was blessed on March 8, 2015 and houses relics of ten saints.
