St. Antony’s Church, Kannamaly is a faith-filled parish with a sacred heritage spanning over 281 years. Rooted in deep devotion and shaped by resilience through centuries of trials, the Church stands today as a living testimony of God’s grace and the loving intercession of St. Antony.

The Kannamaly St. Joseph Nercha Sadya, observed annually on 19th March since 1905, commemorates a miraculous healing from a devastating cholera outbreak and remains a powerful expression of faith and thanksgiving.

The Miraculous Statue of St. Joseph, brought from Spain in 1905, continues to draw lakhs of devotees who seek his powerful intercession.

History of St. Antony’s Church, Kannamaly

The first church at Kannamaly was established in 1745, about 3 km west of the present site, under the patronage of Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception. It was destroyed by sea erosion in 1795, after which a second church was constructed in 1796.

In 1873, Kannamaly was declared an independent parish. Severe sea erosions in 1905 and 1908 caused extensive damage and complete ruin of the church.

The foundation stone for the present church was laid on 13 June 1909. Built in the model of St. Paul’s Basilica, Rome, it was solemnly blessed on 22 November 1942. Though originally dedicated to Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception, it is now revered under the patronage of St. Antony.

History of St. Joseph Nercha Sadya

The Nercha Sadya originated in 1905 during a time of devastating sea erosion and a deadly cholera epidemic. Rev. Fr. Joseph Suarez, the parish priest, received a divine vision of St. Joseph with the Infant Jesus and the Blessed Virgin Mary.

Following this, the faithful observed the Feast of St. Joseph by preparing food in their homes, blessing it at the church, and sharing it with all—including the sick. Miraculously, the cholera outbreak subsided.

Since then, Kannamaly has remained free from cholera, and the Nercha Sadya continues every year on 19th March.

Miraculous Statue of St. Joseph

The statue of St. Joseph was brought from Spain by Carmelite priests in 1905. When the ship reached Kannamaly, it became mysteriously grounded. One statue— with a damaged leg—was unloaded, after which the ship floated free.

Attempts to reload the statue failed, and it was accepted as God’s will that the statue remain at Kannamaly. Later, miraculous healings occurred when straw from the statue’s packing cured sick cattle.

Recognizing its miraculous nature, the statue was installed in the church and continues to bless devotees to this day.

Miraculous Shrine of St. Joseph

A new shrine was envisioned to replace the old chapel built in 1946. The foundation stone was laid on 18 March 2012 by Shri Oommen Chandy and blessed by Bishop Dr. Joseph Kariyil.

The shrine, built over three floors and artistically designed as a blossoming branch held by St. Joseph, houses relics of ten saints.

Solemnly blessed on 8 March 2015, it is the only shrine in Asia enshrining relics of ten saints under one roof.
